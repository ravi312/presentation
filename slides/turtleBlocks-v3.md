## Methodology
<!-- .slide:   data-background="./assets/turtleBlocks/img/project-1.png" -->
<!-- .slide:  style="position: absolute; width: 40%; right: 0; box-shadow: 0 1px 4px rgba(0,0,0,0.5), 0 5px 25px rgba(0,0,0,0.2); background-color: rgba(0, 0, 0, 0.9); color: #fff; padding: 20px; font-size: 18px; text-align: left"-->
- 7-8 yrs(1), 10-12 yrs(2), 14 yrs(1), adult
- Two sessions (2 hr) every week ->  3 weeks
- BBB(synchronous) , IM (asynchronous)
- Design-challenge, Curated [wiki](https://metastudio.org/t/turtleart-challenges-wiki/4036)
- Projects -> blocks -> concepts (Brennan and Resnik, 2012)
> sequences, iterations, loops, data , events, conditionals, operators, and parallelism
- **Data Analysis**
  - Thematic coding -> reflective entries, IM chat, Notes
  - Quantitative analysis ->  Project files

Note:
open invite, voluntary , online  
heterogeneous group of young people and adults   
Grade 4-5 (1), Grade 6-8 (2), Grade 9-12 (1), Adult (1)

## Findings & Discussion
<!-- .slide:   data-background="./assets/turtleBlocks/img/block-frequency-2.png" -->
<!-- .slide:  style="position: absolute; width: 40%; right: 0; box-shadow: 0 1px 4px rgba(0,0,0,0.5), 0 5px 25px rgba(0,0,0,0.2); background-color: rgba(0, 0, 0, 0.9); color: #fff; padding: 20px; font-size: 18px; text-align: left"-->
**Preliminary Insights**
- Preference for certain types of blocks
- Certain programming concepts not very intuitive
- Opportunities for non-linear exploration
- Agency: personally meaningful project challenges
- Different levels of learners in the cohort -> created a ZPD

>Diverse group and open-ended challenges

**Challenges and Limitation**
>- Infrastructural challenges
> - Small sample and limited time frame of three weeks
